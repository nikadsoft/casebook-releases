Casebook

Take control of your court paperwork — scan, organise, search and share your documents, kept privately on your own computer.

Free for Windows, macOS and Linux.

Why I built this

I created this project (with the help of Claude) after going through court four times and finding the court documents overwhelming and difficult to find, reference and remind myself of the content. Casebook is what I built to solve that for myself — and I'm sharing it in case it helps you too.

  • The scanner code is optimised, so hopefully it works smoothly with your scanner.
  • It uses a third-party OCR tool so you can search the documents you've scanned — type a name, date or phrase and jump straight to the page.
  • There's an export facility for sending documents to your solicitor or other third parties.

What it does

📥

Bring everything in

Scan documents, or import PDFs, images, Word files and text — even pull in emails from the people involved in your case.

🔎

Make it searchable

Pages are run through OCR so the text becomes fully searchable. Find anything in seconds, and copy a page's text into another document.

🗂️

Organise your way

Group documents into cases and hearings, classify them by type (statements, orders, CAFCASS, solicitor, social services…), and reorder them.

📖

Read & annotate

A built-in viewer with zoom, rotate, highlights and notes — including notes attached to a specific highlight.

📤

Export & share

Export to a searchable PDF or multi-page image to send on. Share securely to another computer, or sync between your own devices — all end-to-end encrypted.

🔒

Private by design

Everything lives on your machine. No cloud account, and nothing about your case is uploaded to anyone.

Private & secure

Your documents stay yours

There's no sign-up and no cloud. Your cases, documents, notes and search index are stored in a folder on your own computer. Sharing between devices happens directly over your local network and is end-to-end encrypted; email is imported as plain text with no scripts or programs ever run. More on privacy & security →
